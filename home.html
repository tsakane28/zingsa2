ZIMBABWE NATIONAL GEOSPATIAL AND SPACE AGENCY

Zimbabwe National Geospatial and Space Agency, an emblem of innovation and progress nestled in the heart of Harare, Zimbabwe. Established as a statutory body under the Research Act [Chapter 10:22], ZINGSA stands at the forefront of advancing space science and technology within the nation and beyond. With a resolute commitment to promoting the peaceful use of space, ZINGSA spearheads initiatives that span the breadth of geospatial science, earth observation, space engineering, and beyond

At its core, ZINGSA embodies the ethos of exploration and discovery, harnessing the boundless potential of space to address pressing challenges and unlock new horizons of knowledge and opportunity. From satellite communication systems to global navigation satellite systems, the agency's mandate encompasses a spectrum of endeavors aimed at harnessing space technology for the betterment of society, the environment, and scientific understanding.

Headquartered in Harare, Zimbabwe's vibrant capital, ZINGSA serves as a beacon of collaboration and innovation, drawing upon the diverse talents and expertise of its dedicated team of professionals. Together, they embark on a journey of exploration, undertaking projects that push the boundaries of possibility and pave the way for a future where the benefits of space are accessible to all.

As ZINGSA ventures into the cosmos, it not only illuminates the path for Zimbabwe's scientific and technological advancement but also establishes itself as a valued partner in the global pursuit of space exploration and discovery. With vision, determination, and a steadfast commitment to excellence, ZINGSA heralds a new era of space science and technology in Zimbabwe and beyond

ZINGSA's Strides in Precision Agriculture
Science & Innovation - Feb 18

Zimbabwe's National Geospatial and Space Agency (ZINGSA) has made significant strides in the field of precision agriculture, leveraging satellite technology to boost crop yields and improve agricultural productivity. The agency has signed a Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) with the Space Advisory Group to advance precision agriculture initiatives.This collaboration aims to utilize satellites to survey soils and determine specific fertilizers for optimal crop growth, enhancing agricultural productivity and reducing waste.
